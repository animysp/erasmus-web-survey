Purpose of the Survey

This research study investigates how different survey interface designs and presentation methods affect engagement in survey participation, particularly among individuals with mental health concerns. We are examining various aspects of online survey design to understand what makes surveys more accessible and user-friendly.

Data Collection and Uses

During this study, we will collect the following information:

Survey Response Data
  • Demographic information
  • Your responses to the PHQ-9 questionnaire
  • Previous experience with online surveys

Behavioral Data

  • Time spent viewing the survey invitation
  • Click patterns and navigation behavior
  • Completion rate and response time for different sections
  • Device type and browser information

Additional Information
Feedback about your experience with the survey interface

The data will be analyzed to better understand user responses to different design elements and to develop improved survey invitation interfaces for future research.

What is the PHQ-9?

The Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9) is a widely-used, validated screening tool for depression. It consists of 9 questions that ask about symptoms you may have experienced over the past two weeks. In this study, this questionnaire is used for research purposes only and does not constitute a clinical diagnosis. Your responses help researchers understand mental health patterns in the general population.

Confidentiality

All information you provide will be kept confidential and will be accessible only to the researchers involved in this project. Your personal information and demographic details will be fully anonymized. The results of the study will be reported without any personal identification.

Data Retention

During the study, all data will be stored on secure systems. Electronic data will be stored in a secure cloud storage, accessible only through a password-protected account known only to the research team. Any paper-based materials will be stored in a secure location, with access limited to the research team. All data will be retained for at least 4 years for the purpose of verification or reference if necessary.
